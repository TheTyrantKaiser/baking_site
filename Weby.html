How to make a Brioche Bread

The Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of warm milk
  • 1 packet of instant dry yeast or 2 1/2 tsp
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar (divided)
  • 6 tbsp of unsalted butter
  • 2 eggs room temp
  • 2 1/2 cups of all purpose flour

    Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine 1/2 cup of warm milk, 1 packet (2 1/2 tsp) of instant dry yeast, and 1 tbsp of granulated sugar (remove 1 tbsp from the 1/4 cup of sugar). Let the mixture sit until the yeast becomes active, and foamy
  2. Add in the rest of the sugar, 6 tbsps of butter, and 2 eggs. Then whisk until everything comes together. If you're using a stand mixer then whisk on medium high speed.
  3. Add 2 cups of flour, and 1 tsp of salt into the bowl. Use a fork (or switch out the whisk for a dough hook if using a stand mixer), mix (at low speed with dough hook) until the flour is fully incorporated. You can determine if you need more flour at this point, but when you're done the right consistency should pull clean from the mixer, it shouldn't be too stiff, and slightly sticky.
  4. Then increase the speed to a medium high speed, and mix for an additional 3-4 mintues (if you don't have a mixer with a dough hook then continue to mix for 15 minutes or more).
  5. Remove the dough from the mixer, and place it into a oiled bowl (or utilise the same bowl), cover it with plastic wrap, and keep it covered for about an hour until the dough doubles in size.
  6. Once the dough has doubled in size, fold it by tipping the dough out onto a lightly floured surface, and pound the air out of it. Roll it back into a ball, and place it into your bowl, cover, and let it rest for another 30-45 minutes. It should up again, and be airy but not quite double in size.
  7. Prepare a loaf pan (it can be the aluminum version) by spraying it with nonstick baking spray, and set aside
  8. When your dough is ready tip it out onto a lightly floured surface again, and pat flat. Cut the dough into 3 equal pieces, and roll it out to about 12 inches (or approximately 9 if that's the size of your loaf pan). Then lay each strand of dough side by side, and join the top together by lightly rolling them into a point. Braid the strands all the way down and at the bottom pinch, and roll the ends together.
  9. Tuck the pointed ends under the loaf, and fit the loaf into the prepareed pan. Cover (with plastic wrap) and let rest for about 30-45 minutes. Until the dough has risen slightly above the top of the pan.
  10. While the loaf is proofing, preheat your oven to 325 degrees F (165 C)
  11. Once your loaf is proofed, and ready, uncover and brush with your egg wash (1 egg, and water). Bake for 30-45 minutes. The loaf will be golden brown, and a thermometer inserted into the center of the loaf should read 190 degrees F.

How to create your very own Bitter & Blanc

The Ingredients

  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 1 pint of heavy cream (divided)
  • 2 1/2 tsp of vanilla extract (divided)
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tsp of corn starch
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 6 tbsp butter
  • 1 big loaf of Brioche bread (can use less)
  • 1 cup of white chocolate chips
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • Souffle Cup(s)

    Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 C)
  2. Slice (or take) a piece off of your Brioche bread, remove the crusts of the bread, and dice it into squares, then put the diced pieces into a large bowl, and pour heavy cream over the bread (the amount you pour isn't relevant to the 1 pint of heavy cream, as long as it covers / soaks the bread); let the heavy cream soak in.
  3. In the meantime create your ganache by heating 1 1/2 cups of heavy cream to a simmer, then pour the heavy cream over the chocolate chips, and add 1/2 vanilla extract, stir until smooth.
  4. Now for the bread pudding. in a pan (preferrably a non-stick pan) melt the 1 cup of white chocolate chips with 6tbsp of butter, and set aside.
  5. In a mixing bowl, add 1/2 heavy cream, 3 tbsp of sugar, 3 large egg yolks, and add the melted white chocolate / butter, and mix on a slow speed.
  6. After mixing, add the cream soaked bread, and mix well.

    The Vanilla Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, put in 2 egg yolks, and 1 tsp of corn starch.
  2. boil 1 cup of whole milk, and 2 tbsp of sugar in a pot.
  3. pour the milk into the small bowl, and whisk, then add 1 tsp of vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the mixture into the same pot, and cook on low heat until it reaches a coating consistency (it should coat the front, and back of your spoon when dipped), then pour it through a strainer to remove the lumps.

    Baking

  1. Grease the souffle cup(s) with butter, and sprinkle lightly with 1 tsp of sugar.
  2. Using a spoon pour a layer of chocolate ganache into the bottom of the souffle cup.
  3. Then add the bread pudding mixture, and bake at 325 degrees F (165 C) for 45 minutes.

    Serving

  1. After the bread pudding is done baking, let it cool slightly, and dust it with powdered sugar, and serve with vanilla sauce, and ice cream
